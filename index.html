ORIGAMI DESIGNS AND SOME FACTS

LEARNING ORIGAMI



Camel

  1. CAMEL'S EAR ARE VERY FURRY.
  2. CAMELS CAN MOVE EASILY ON SAND BECAUSE THEY HAVE SPECIALLY DESIGNED FEETS.

TEDDY BEAR

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall! It was created by South African artist Cheryl Moss who is known for her minuscule work.

PINGEON

  1. Pigeons have fought alongside humans in World War I and II.
  2. Each wing of pigeon has 23 remiges which remain attached to its hinder border.

PANDA

  1. Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  2. They spend a lot of their day eating.
  3. Bamboo is critical to their diet.

CHAMELEON

  1. THEY MAINLY CHANGE COLOR IN ORDER TO COMMUNICATE OR REGULATE BODY TEMPERATURE.
  2. SKIN CRYSTALS ENABLE THEM TO CHANGE COLOR AT WILL.

FLYING CICADA

  1. Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans.
  2. Cicadas inundate forests as a survival mechanism.

FROG

  1. Frogs don't need to drink water as they absorb it through their skin.
  2. A frog's call is unique to its species, and some frog calls can be heard up to a mile away.

DOG

  1. Their sense of smell is at least 40x better than ours.
  2. Their sense of smell is at least 40x better than ours.
